How CatfishGuard Works

Our AI examines seven different characteristics of an image to determine if it was generated by artificial intelligence rather than captured by a real camera.

Anatomical Anomalies

Detects warped ears, twisted fingers, mismatched eyes, and distorted facial boundaries that AI generators frequently produce.

Background Errors

Spots warped lines, melting objects, and incoherent backgrounds where spatial relationships make no physical sense.

"Too Perfect" Skin

Identifies glassy, airbrushed skin lacking pores, blemishes, and the micro-texture that all real human skin has.

Text & Object Issues

Catches garbled text, scrambled letters, and broken object boundaries where things improperly merge together.

Lighting Mismatches

Detects when shadows point different directions or the subject is lit differently from the background — physically impossible in real photos.

Frequency Domain

Examines frequency signatures — real cameras produce high-frequency sensor detail that AI cannot replicate.

Noise Analysis

Checks for natural camera sensor noise that varies with brightness. AI images have zero noise or artificial uniform noise.
